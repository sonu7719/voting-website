BJP (Bharatiya Janata Party)

The Bharatiya Janata Party is one of the major political parties in India. It was founded in 1980 and is often associated with the ideology of Hindutva or Hindu nationalism. BJP's political agenda includes a focus on economic reforms, national security, cultural nationalism, and promoting traditional Indian values. It has been in power multiple times both at the national level and in various states across India. Currently, it leads the National Democratic Alliance (NDA) coalition and holds power at the center.

Congress (Indian National Congress)

The Indian National Congress is one of the oldest and historically significant political parties in India. It played a crucial role in India's independence movement and was the dominant political force in the country post-independence. Congress has a broad-based agenda that includes social justice, secularism, and economic development. It has been in power for significant periods at the national level and in various states, but its influence has declined in recent years, particularly after the rise of the BJP.

AAP (Aam Aadmi Party)

The Aam Aadmi Party is a relatively newer political party in India, formed in 2012. It emerged from the anti-corruption movement led by activist Arvind Kejriwal. AAP's core principles include transparency in governance, anti-corruption measures, and a focus on issues affecting the common people ("aam aadmi"). It gained significant traction in Delhi, winning a landslide victory in the 2015 Delhi Legislative Assembly elections and subsequently forming the government in the state.

Shiv Sena

Shiv Sena is a regional political party based primarily in the state of Maharashtra. It was founded in 1966 by Bal Thackeray. The party originally espoused Marathi regionalism and Hindu nationalism. However, its ideology has evolved over time to include a broader platform encompassing issues such as regional development and welfare. Shiv Sena has been a significant player in Maharashtra politics and has formed alliances with various other parties at different points in time. It currently shares power in Maharashtra as part of the Maha Vikas Aghadi coalition.
